Visit Us – Understanding Bouldering

Bouldering Basics

Bouldering is a form of rock climbing that is meant to be done on small (relative to cliff faces and mountains) rock formations and/or artificial rock walls like the ones we have in our gym. Bouldering is inherently a dangerous sport that does NOT involve the use of any ropes or harnesses.

  • Bouldering routes are typically 15–20 feet high. Our walls are 13 feet tall on average.
  • Crash pads are used for safety. All walls in our gym have pads installed beneath them.
  • Climbing shoes and chalk provide grip and hygiene. We use liquid chalk and chalk socks only—no loose or block chalk allowed.

Bouldering Etiquette

Safety First:

  • Mind fall zones: Never walk or stand under climbers.
  • Keep pads clear: No personal items or water bottles on padded areas.
  • Spotting: Only spot when needed to prevent injury (e.g., avoiding walls or pad misses).

Climbing in a Shared Space:

  • Wait your turn if someone is climbing near your route.
  • Give climbers space to climb and fall safely.
  • Observe respectfully. Avoid shouting beta unless asked for.
Don’t: Say, “Bump your left hand up.” Do: Encourage them: “Come on, you got this!”
  • Limit yourself to two consecutive attempts per climb to let others have turns.

Being Considerate:

  • Ask politely for advice or offer help only when requested.
  • Respect climbers at all levels. Avoid showing off or boasting.
  • Clean up after yourself—chalk, trash, gear, etc.
